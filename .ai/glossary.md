## Business Terms

### Backtest
**Definition**: The process of evaluating a trading strategy by applying it to historical stock price data to calculate theoretical returns.
**Evidence**: `README.md:3` — "professional full-stack application for backtesting stock trading signals"

### Signal
**Definition**: A stock symbol paired with a signal date, representing a potential buy recommendation generated by a screener.
**Evidence**: `backend/main.py:89` — `signals = df.to_dict(orient="records")`, each record is a `{symbol, date}` pair

### Holding Period / Horizon
**Definition**: The number of calendar days a position is held after entry. The system computes returns at 7, 14, 30, 45, 60, and 90 days.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:201` — `horizons = sorted(list(set([7, 14, 30, 45, 60, 90, duration])))`

### Win Rate
**Definition**: The percentage of signals that produced a positive return over a given holding period.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:255` — `win_rate = round((len([x for x in rets if x > 0]) / len(rets)) * 100, 2)`

### Duration
**Definition**: The maximum holding period in days (default 90). Clamped to `[7, 180]`.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:12,15` — `duration: int = 90`, `duration = min(max(duration, 7), 180)`

### Entry Price
**Definition**: The closing price on the first available trading day on or after the signal date (up to 5 days lookahead).
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:178,188` — `entry_date = get_next_trading_day(signal_date, df)`, `entry_price = df.loc[entry_date]["Close"]`

### Exit Price
**Definition**: The closing price on the first available trading day on or after the target date (signal_date + horizon days).
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:206-210` — `target_date = entry_date + timedelta(days=h)`, `exit_date = get_next_trading_day(target_date, df)`, `exit_price = df.loc[exit_date]["Close"]`

### Max High / Max Low
**Definition**: The highest High price and lowest Low price observed within the duration window after entry.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:224-235` — `window_df[entry_date:window_end]`, `.max()`, `.min()`

### Profit Factor
**Definition**: Gross profit divided by gross loss. Computed client-side in the Dashboard.
**Evidence**: `frontend/src/components/Dashboard.jsx:49-50` — `profitFactor = grossLoss > 0 ? (grossProfit / grossLoss) : (grossProfit > 0 ? Infinity : 0)`

### Sector Edge
**Definition**: Average 30-day return grouped by sector, requiring a minimum of 3 trades per sector.
**Evidence**: `frontend/src/components/Dashboard.jsx:76-107` — `enrichmentStats`, `if item.count >= 3`

### Capital Return
**Definition**: The average return percentage multiplied by a user-defined capital amount.
**Evidence**: `frontend/src/components/Dashboard.jsx:63` — `capitalReturn: capital * (avg / 100)`

## Domain Concepts

### Phase A — Resolution
**Definition**: First phase of the backtester: extracts symbols, resolves to Yahoo Finance tickers, parses dates, computes global date bounds.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:17-75` — comment `# --- Phase A: Resolution & Bounds Calculation ---`

### Phase B — Bulk Fetch & Enrichment
**Definition**: Second phase: single `yf.download()` call for all unique symbols, plus concurrent metadata (sector, marketCap) fetches bounded by `Semaphore(10)`.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:77-105` — comment `# --- Phase B: Bulk Fetching & Enrichment ---`

### Phase C — Calculation Loop
**Definition**: Third phase: slices the bulk DataFrame per symbol, calculates returns at each horizon, computes max high/low, aggregates into BacktestReport.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:107-237` — comment `# --- Phase C: Calculation Loop ---`

### Bulk Fetch
**Definition**: A single `yf.download(group_by='ticker')` call for all unique symbols at once, returning a `pd.MultiIndex` DataFrame. Not cached.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/data_provider.py:37-53` — comment: "Does not use diskcache for the bulk blob"

### Fallback / Sequential Fetch
**Definition**: Individual `yf.Ticker(symbol).history(start, end)` call triggered when a symbol is missing from the bulk DataFrame.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:154-162` — `[FALLBACK]` print, calls `DataProvider.get_ticker_data`

### Enrichment / Metadata
**Definition**: Fetching `sector` and `marketCap` from `yf.Ticker(symbol).info` for each unique symbol. Cached for 7 days.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/data_provider.py:56-82` — `get_ticker_info`, `cache.set(cache_key, result, expire=604800)`

### Symbol Resolution
**Definition**: Converting a bare stock name (e.g., `RELIANCE`) to a Yahoo Finance ticker by trying `.NS` (NSE) then `.BO` (BSE) suffixes.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/symbol_resolver.py:29-47` — `_resolve_uncached`, tries NSE first, then BSE

### NSE (National Stock Exchange)
**Definition**: India's primary stock exchange. Tickers use `.NS` suffix on Yahoo Finance.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/symbol_resolver.py:37-39` — `ns_symbol = f"{symbol}.NS"`

### BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange)
**Definition**: India's oldest stock exchange. Tickers use `.BO` suffix on Yahoo Finance.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/symbol_resolver.py:42-45` — `bo_symbol = f"{symbol}.BO"`

### OHLCV
**Definition**: Open, High, Low, Close, Volume — the standard columns returned by Yahoo Finance for historical price data.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:188,210,228-229,232-233` — uses `Close`, `High`, `Low` columns

### MultiIndex DataFrame
**Definition**: A pandas DataFrame with hierarchical column index (ticker level 0, OHLCV column level 1), returned by `yf.download(group_by='ticker')` when 2+ symbols are fetched.
**Evidence**: `backend/core/backtester.py:140-142` — `isinstance(bulk_df.columns, pd.MultiIndex)`, `bulk_df[resolved_symbol]`

### Next Trading Day
**Definition**: The nearest trading day on or after a given date, found by scanning up to 5 days forward in the price DataFrame index.
**Evidence**: `backend/utils/date_utils.py:8-16` — `get_next_trading_day`, `max_lookahead: int = 5`
